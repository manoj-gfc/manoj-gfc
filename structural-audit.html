Why Structural Audit is Essential for Old Buildings

Published on April 25, 2026 | By Manoj Kumar

What is a Structural Audit?

A structural audit is a thorough inspection of a building's structural health. It identifies potential weaknesses, cracks, corrosion, foundation settlement, and other risks that could compromise safety. For buildings older than 15–20 years, regular audits are not just recommended – they are essential.

Why Your Building Needs a Structural Audit

  • Safety First: Prevents sudden collapses and protects lives.
  • Legal Compliance: Many municipalities now mandate audits for older buildings.
  • Increased Lifespan: Early detection allows timely repairs, extending building life.
  • Higher Property Value: A certified safe building commands better resale value.
  • Insurance Benefits: Some insurers offer lower premiums for audited buildings.

Signs You Need an Audit Immediately

  • Cracks in walls, beams, or columns
  • Dampness or water leakage
  • Peeling plaster or paint
  • Uneven floors or sagging roofs
  • Corrosion of steel reinforcement
